Schema: `feature` · `archived` · `accuracy (M/N)` · `Citation resolve-rate (M/N)` · `only-required-changes (M/N)` · `notes`.

The **Citation resolve-rate** column is the `scripts/check-citations.sh` resolve-rate: it proves each `path:line` citation still points at a real line, not that the quoted text is intact (`scripts/check-citations.sh:12-13`). It is a repo-wide **proxy** for "proven facts with sources" — named for exactly what it measures, not for a stronger claim it cannot support.

**Read the Citation resolve-rate column as a repo-level trend, never as a per-cycle score.** It is measured across the whole repo at the moment of harvest, so rows harvested close together carry the same value by construction and the column shows no per-feature variation. A cycle that added no citations and a cycle that added fifty will record identically. The two other columns are genuinely per-cycle; this one is a snapshot of the repo that happens to be stamped on the row.
